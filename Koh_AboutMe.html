About Me

"What do you regret most in life?"

I've been asking this question to classmates, adults in the workforce, and senior citizens for a long time. Although their answers are specific and vary widely, they mostly boil down to one grievance: missed opportunities. As an aspiring software engineer and mechanical engineering enthusiast, I aim to make the most out of field trips, volunteering opportunities, and personal projects in order to achieve my goals. This means that time and effort must be put in, and a quality product should come out of the experience. Ensuring that I make the most out of every experience eliminates the problem of missed opportunities while also allowing me to learn skills that are invaluable in the workforce.

I am experienced, charismatic, and self-motivated. As a senior at Dublin High school and president of the robotics club, I have a responsibility to lead and set a standard for my peers. My experience in the building and coding aspects of robotics has allowed me to acquire an understanding of science and math not taught in schools. My charisma as a team leader holds our robotics team together, especially when we are all stressed out. My self-motivation dictates my time management of robotics, homework, and extracurricular activities such that everything is completed according to schedule while still allowing me to sleep at a reasonable hour. These traits have, and will continue to benefit me in my exploration of computer science and mechanical engineering.

Throughout my experiences as a robotics captain, I have learned that skill can always be overcome by effort. As a captain with only two years of experience in robotics, I have been overshadowed by other captains who have been working in the field for much longer. However, knowing that I had less skill motivated me to spend more time and effort into construction and optimization of the robot. My robot performed at the same level, if not better, than other robots in the competition, proving that my effort had levelled the playing field.

My interest lies in the implementation of code and algorithms into mechanical structures. As a result, I have been constantly carrying out personal projects which involve designing a mechanism, linking it to a microprocessor, and programming it in order to fulfil a certain task or function. I aspire to harness my knowledge of math, science and critical thinking in order to push the limits of what robots and machines can achieve. Design of a mechanism and creation of an algorithm both require sophisticated yet intuitive applications of mathematics and science. Critical thinking plays a vital role in both prototyping and troubleshooting a system. Combined, these attributes form the basis of a successful design. If you would like to find out about how I integrate these qualities into my work, please feel free to explore my "Projects" and "Work Experience" pages on this site.
